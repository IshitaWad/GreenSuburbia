Tips and Tricks for aspiring conservationists

Electricity Conservation Tips

If our calculator let you know that your highest carbon footprint comes from electricity consumption, here are some helpful tips for you

1. If you have electric heat, lower your thermostat by two degrees to save 5% on your heating bill. Lowering it five degrees could save 10%.

2. Keep your fridge and freezer at their ideal temperature. For your fridge this is between 2°C and 3°C and your freezer should be at -18°C.

3. Turn off ceiling lights and use table lamps, track lighting and under-counter lights in work and hobby areas as well as in kitchens.

4. Two 100-watt incandescent bulbs switched off an extra two hours per day could save you $15 over a year. Better yet, switch to LED.

5. Upgrades to the third component of an HVAC system – ventilation – can also improve your energy efficiency. Proper insulation and maintenance on your ventilation system can reduce your heating and cooling expenses by up to 20%.

Water Conservation Tips

If our calculator let you know that your highest carbon footprint comes from water consumption, here are some helpful tips for you

1. Try to keep your showering between 5-10 minutes. Remember every minute you reduce, can save you up to 10.61 kg of CO2

2. Use a dishwasher rather than hand washing your dishes.

3. Wash as much clothing as possible in one load.

4. Stick to showers rather than long baths.

5. Check your house for leaks in taps, both inside and outside regularly.

Natural Gas Conservation Tips

If our calculator let you know that your highest carbon footprint comes from natural gas consumption, here are some helpful tips for you

1. If you can afford it, using a hybrid car that uses electricity rather than other forms of gas can help cut down emissions significantly

2. Regularly service your car to keep it more efficient.

3. Check your tires. Keeping tires pumped correctly can reduce emissions. “Low tire pressure will hurt your fuel economy,” Mr. West said.

4. Air conditioning and intensive city driving can make emissions creep up. Cut down on these as often as possible.

5. Use cruise control on long drives — in most cases, this can help to save gas.

6. Don’t weigh your car down with extra things that you don’t need on your trip.
